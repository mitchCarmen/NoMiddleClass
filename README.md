An interactive data presentation examining 100 years of wealth inequality in America, the explosion of CEO pay, and what a healthier distribution could look like.

### Wealth Concentration
- **Top 1%** now holds **30.5%** of all US wealth (Q1 2024)
- **Bottom 50%** (165 million Americans) hold just **2.5%** of wealth
- The top 0.1% wealth share has returned to 1929 levels (~22%)

### CEO Pay Explosion
- **1965**: CEOs earned 21x typical worker pay
- **2024**: CEOs earn 281x typical worker pay
- CEO pay has grown **1,094%** since 1978; worker pay only **26%**

### The "Golden Age" (1940s-1970s)
- Bottom 90% wealth share peaked at **35%** in mid-1980s
- Has since fallen back to **~24%**
- Strong unions, progressive taxation, and homeownership programs built the middle class
